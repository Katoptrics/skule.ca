Cheque Requisitions

\r\n

What is a Cheque Requisition?

\r\n

A cheque requisition is a form that is filed to receive reimbursement for expenses paid on behalf of your\xc2\xa0affiliated group (i.e. Engineering Society associated entity, directorship or affiliated club). You must attach original receipts with your cheque requisition. You can attach multiple receipts to your form because each line item on the form is for a different receipt. An example form is here. Please note each line item would have a corresponding receipt submitted with the form.

\r\n\r\n

Where Can I Get a Form?

\r\n

You can\xc2\xa0pick up a copy of the form in the Engineering Society office or print one from the following link.

\r\n\r\n

Where Do I Submit My Form?

\r\n

Forms can be submitted\xc2\xa0as a hard copy to the Engineering Society office. The VP Finance will review your form, and the\xc2\xa0business manager will email you once your cheque is ready.\r\nYou can also email a scanned or digital copy of your cheque requisition forms to vpfinance@skule.ca and CC rhonda@skule.ca. You must also scan and send copies of your receipts with the form AND save the original for collection at a later date. Your reimbursement can then be e-transferred to you at the email you provided on the form. \r\n\r\n

\r\n

Do's and Don'ts of a Cheque Requisition

\r\n
    \r\n
  1. Don't: Purchase EngSoc items and personal items in the same transaction. It makes tallying the\xc2\xa0final approved amount much more complicated, and it is very easy to ask a cashier to do two\xc2\xa0separate transactions for you. It will save both you and the VP Finance a lot of headaches if your\xc2\xa0receipts are for EngSoc items only!
    2. \r\n
  2. Don't: Leave submissions to the last minute.
    3. \r\n
  3. Do: Double check your cheque requisition form before submitting. Submitting a cheque\xc2\xa0requisition with addition errors or missing information will delay the process of issuing you your\xc2\xa0reimbursement cheque, which you do not want!
    4. \r\n
  4. Do: Attach original receipts. For auditing purposes, all original receipts MUST be attached to\xc2\xa0your cheque requisition form.
    5. \r\n
  5. Do: Write clearly. Having legible handwriting means there will be no confusion with the amount\xc2\xa0requested or the contact email on the form.
    6. \r\n
  6. Do: Ask questions if you are unsure where to put an expense on the cheque requisition form.\xc2\xa0Things like gas charges do not go under "Tips or Other", this is a regular line item just like any\xc2\xa0other expense.
    7. \r\n
\r\n\r\n

Contact

\r\n

If you have any questions about the cheque requisition process, please contact the VP Finance at vpfinance@skule.ca.
